Your Financial Profile

How do I estimate my post-tax income?

Use your take-home pay, not your salary. The easiest way: look at your pay stub and multiply your net deposit by the number of pay periods per year. This already accounts for income tax, CPP/QPP, and EI deductions.

Federal public servants can estimate net pay with fedpay.ca (independent, not affiliated).

Enter only employment income here — investment and rental income belong in the Investment Portfolio section so they aren't double-counted.

I'm part of a couple — what do I enter?

Tick Planning as a couple to open the Spouse / Partner Profile box. You can enter each partner's employment income, retirement age, DB pension, and CPP/OAS independently on their own timelines.
